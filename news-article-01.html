Beyond Salt Spray: The Hidden Risk of HASCC in High-Strength Fasteners

By Fasto Engineering Lab | Updated July 2026

If you've ever seen a bolt head "pop off" several days after installation, you didn't have a torque problem. You had a metallurgy problem. In the industry, we call it **HASCC** (Hydrogen Assisted Stress Corrosion Cracking). It is the single most common cause of catastrophic failure in industrial metal roofing.

The Standard Screw Trap

Standard case-hardened carbon steel screws (Class 12.9 equivalents) are brittle by nature. When exposed to moisture, atomic hydrogen penetrates the steel lattice. Under static tension, these hydrogen atoms migrate to stress points, causing the internal structure to snap without warning. Plating only delays the inevitable; it doesn't solve the chemistry.

"A4 Bimetal technology isn't an upgrade; it's insurance. It eliminates the cathode-anode reaction that feeds HASCC."

How Fasto BF Series Fixes This

Our BF3 and BF6 series utilize **Precise Friction Welding**. By welding a 304 or 316 stainless steel shank to a hardened SCM435 drill point, the part of the screw that stays under stress is immune to hydrogen embrittlement. The "dangerous" carbon steel is limited strictly to the drill point, which clears the substrate and carries no load after installation.

Technical Checklist for EPC Contractors:

  • Verify the Junction: Inspect the welding line. It should be a clean, molecular fusion, not a messy braze.
  • Material Traceability: Always request PMI (Positive Material Identification) reports to confirm Ni and Cr content in the SUS316 body.
  • Drill Point Geometry: Ensure the flute length is greater than the total substrate thickness to avoid torque spikes.
