The Calculus of Fastening:
Don't Let Your Threads Engage Too Early

By Fasto Engineering Team | July 2026

It’s the most common field failure in metal roofing: a screw snaps its head off mid-installation. 90% of the time, the installer blames the metallurgy. 90% of the time, the installer is wrong. The real culprit? **Incorrect length selection leading to premature thread engagement.**

The "Point vs. Plate" Physics

For a bimetal screw to work correctly, the drill point (the hardened carbon steel part) must fully penetrate the steel substrate **before** the leading thread of the stainless steel body makes contact.

If your screw is too short, or your drill point is too small (#3 instead of #5), the threads will start trying to "pull" the screw through the steel while the point is still "drilling" it. This creates a massive torque spike that even the best friction weld can't survive. It’s not a quality issue; it’s a math issue.

The Fasto Formula for Length (L):

L = T(fix) + T(sub) + L(point) + 5mm
  • T(fix): Thickness of the material being fixed (e.g., sandwich panel).
  • T(sub): Thickness of the structural steel substrate.
  • L(point): Length of the drill point (must clear the substrate).
  • 5mm: The standard "3-thread" protrusion requirement.

Specific Case: The BF3-12 Advantage

When you are dealing with a 10mm or 12mm hot-rolled steel purlin, you cannot use a standard screw. You need the **BF3-12**. Why? Because its #5 point is specifically elongated to ensure the point clears that 12mm plate entirely before the threads engage. Choosing a shorter screw to "save money" is the fastest way to double your labor costs in failed removals.

Pro Tips for EPC Managers:

  • Account for Insulation: If you use compressible glass wool, use the *uncompressed* height for your T(fix) calculation.
  • Thread Pitch Matters: Coarser threads pull faster. If you use a high-speed driver, add an extra 2mm to your safety margin.
  • Check the Burrs: In heavy structural steel, the burr on the exit side can be 1-2mm. Your screw must be long enough to clear it.
