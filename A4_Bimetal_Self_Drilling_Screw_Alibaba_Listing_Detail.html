TRIPLE-BARRIER SHIELD VS GALVANIC & ATMOSPHERIC CORROSION
AS 3566 Class 5 Certified | 1500 Hours Neutral Salt Spray Proof

The induction-hardened SCM435 drill tip is highly crucial for drilling power but highly vulnerable to rusting. FASTO solves this by applying a multi-layer ceramic-organic Ruspert barrier over the entire fastener.

  • 1. SACRIFICIAL METALLIC ZINC LAYER (底层电镀锌牺牲阳极保护):
    Acts as a sacrificial cathode. If the fastener suffers scratch damage during installation, the electroplated zinc layer corrodes preferentially, preventing the carbon steel core from forming structural red rust.
  • 2. CHEMICAL CONVERSION FILM (中间致密化学转化钝化膜):
    Seals micro-pores and halts oxidation on the zinc surface, preventing rapid moisture and oxygen infiltration.
  • 3. CERAMIC ORGANIC-INORGANIC BAKED TOPCOAT (外层杂化陶瓷烘烤面漆):
    Formulated with ceramic-like inorganic particles dispersed in an organic hybrid thermosetting resin. Baked at high temperature to form an exceptionally tough physical barrier that resists UV radiation, acid rain (SO2), and aggressive chemical fumes.
MITIGATION OF GALVANIC (DISSIMILAR METAL) CORROSION

In coastal roof construction, attaching stainless steel screws to structural carbon steel purlins normally induces galvanic corrosion due to a potential difference of ΔV ≈ 0.5 V to 0.6 V.

FASTO's highly resistant Ruspert coating acts as a physical electrical insulator, blocking direct conductive contact, keeping galvanic current near zero, and safeguarding both the roof cladding and the structural joint.

Part 4: COMPLIANCE WARNINGS & OPERATIONS SUMMARY (运营施工规范要点与强制免责声明)

4.1 Professional Installation Quick Guidelines

To guarantee the structural integrity of the joint and maximize the corrosion barrier, installers must adhere strictly to the following parameters:

  1. Tool Specification: Use a specialized high-torque screw gun (not an impact wrench or hammer drill) equipped with a depth-sensitive clutch and a 5/16” magnetic hex socket.
  2. Speed Regulation: Keep the rotation speed between 1500 RPM and 2500 RPM (typical 2000 RPM). Excessive speed (> 3000 RPM) will burn the carbon steel drill point due to frictional heat, while insufficient speed (< 1000 RPM) will fail to supply enough axial pressure to cut the 12 mm steel efficiently.
  3. Washer Compression Control: Tighten until the EPDM rubber layer is compressed to approximately 60–80% of its original uncompressed thickness. The EPDM should form a slight, visible bulge around the perimeter of the stainless backing plate, creating an airtight, watertight seal. Avoid over-tightening (which causes the EPDM to extrude outwards, splitting the rubber and losing UV protection) and under-tightening (which fails to seal water).
